The Food Facts

Fruits

Fruits provide us with water, vitamins, minerals, fiber and different beneficial compounds for the body. Due to its antioxidants, they prevent the premature aging of cells, giving you cleaner, younger, smoother and healthier skin and a higher quality of life.

Consumed daily, fruits help us prevent various diseases such as cardiovascular problems, digestive disorders, some types of cancer and neurodegenerative diseases, in addition to collaborating in the fight against overweight and obesity.

Main Facts

They are sweet, tasty, have few calories and practically no saturated fat (those that are most harmful to the body), so they are a good treat when your sweet tooth comes in.

Top Picks

  • Orange: Source of vitamin C, fiber and folates;
  • Banana: The choice for potassium;
  • Apple: High in antioxidants, low in calories.

Good to know

Fruits give us a feeling of satiety by containing fiber, so you will avoid pecking at kindergartens all day. In addition, its fiber helps us regulate intestinal transit and prevent constipation.

Vegetables

Vegetables provide many benefits from a nutritional point of view and preventing diseases. They are especially important because they regulate intestinal transit and because of the vitamins they provide modulate many metabolic processes

All vegetables have a high percentage of water, and also stand out for their content of carbohydrates, minerals and vitamins. However, they have very little protein and fat. Until recently, and due to the low caloric and protein intake of vegetables, they were considered as food products of relative interest. After the discovery of vitamins, these foods have become an important place in man's nutrition.

Main Facts

Today it is known that more than half of vitamin A and practically all the vitamin C that we need, is provided by vegetables, as well as significant amounts of iron and calcium. In addition, it is necessary to highlight the importance given to dietary fiber, a component of vegetables, lacking in nutritional value but of great interest for digestion.

Top Picks

  • Brocoli: Large amounts of vitamin K and vitamin C;
  • Tomatoe: Contain lycopene, a powerful antioxidant that plays a role in cancer prevention;
  • Onion: The choice for vitamin C, B6 and Manganese.

Good to know

Vegetables and greens lose many of the nutrients they possess when cooked. This loss is mainly due to oxidation on contact with air, high temperatures, long cooking times and dissolution in the cooking liquid. Whenever possible, vegetables should be cooked in the oven with their skin, and when it is necessary to cut them, it will be done in large portions to achieve the smallest surface of contact with water. The amount of water will be the minimum possible and, if possible, a closed container will be used

Protein

Protein is a nutrient needed by the human body for growth and maintenance. Aside from water, proteins are the most abundant kind of molecules in the body. It can be found in all cells of the body and is the major structural component of all cells in the body, especially muscle. This also includes body organs, hair and skin.

Proteins are also used in membranes, such as glycoproteins. When broken down into amino acids, they are used as precursors to nucleic acid, co-enzymes, hormones, immune response, cellular repair, and other molecules essential for life. Additionally, protein is needed to form blood cells.

Main Facts

The most important aspect and defining characteristic of protein from a nutritional standpoint is its amino acid composition. There are multiple systems which rate proteins by their usefulness to an organism based on their relative percentage of amino acids and, in some systems, the digestibility of the protein source.

Top Picks

  • Fish: Average 23% of protein and low fat;
  • Whole grains: Most of the protein are in the germ and bran;
  • Dairy: Not only protein, but also rich in calcium.

Good to know

The lack of energy in the body leads to starvation, the body will use protein from the muscle mass to meet its energy needs, leading to muscle wasting over time. If the individual does not consume adequate protein in nutrition, then muscle will also waste as more vital cellular processes recycle muscle protein for their own requirements.

Grains

The basic food for a large part of the population and one of the ones that provides the most energy due to its richness in carbohydrates. Cereals should be consumed daily and are one of the most complete foods.

Cereals provide energy thanks to the large amount of carbohydrates, especially in the form of starch, that they contain. However, the more refined the cereals, the less nutritional value they have. Therefore, they are considered the most complete foods after milk, apart from being considered basic due to their availability and low cost.

Main Facts

Rich in protein, B vitamins (thiamine, riboflavin and niacin fundamentally), and fiber. They also contain a small proportion of minerals such as phosphorous and potassium, and to a lesser extent, magnesium, calcium and iron. And a small proportion of fats, mainly unsaturated fatty acids.

Top Picks

  • Quinoa: A must for Omega 6 and 3 acids and less carbohydrates;
  • Oatmeal: The one in fiber and unsaturated fats;
  • Rice: Complex carbohydrates and simply no fat.

Good to know

Due to food shortages among the most vulnerable rural populations, their diet is based on the intake of a single cereal (corn or rice) and a legume. The combination is very interesting, since the results of many studies have shown that these two types of food complement each other nutritionally.
